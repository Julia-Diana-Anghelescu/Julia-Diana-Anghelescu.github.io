Project Overview

Problem Statement

Group trip planning can feel like a lonely and maddening internal dialog: where to go, who can afford it, expectations, vibes, and timing. A simpler way to consolidate vacation planning is needed. Wouldn't it be nice to structure this babble with a supportive AI?

Roles and Responsibilities

Our quartet was comprised of a designer, a UX writer, a programmer, and a research enthusiast. We each touched on all aspects of the project. In order to collaborate we leaned on our passions and professional backgrounds.

My main contributions were:

  • Research structure, survey creation, and interviews.
  • Ideating solutions to the core problems.
  • Creating icons and shared components for the entire team.
  • Wireframing and usability testing.
  • Writing developer notes for the final deliverable.

Summary

Our Mission

Empowering group organizers to effortlessly plan unforgettable adventures, while generating shareable snapshots and ensuring a fun-filled, structured travel experience.

Our Vision

To revolutionize group travel planning by:

  • Offering a user-centric platform that prioritizes fun
  • Eliminating planning burdens
  • Providing easily shareable snapshots for coordination

Our Values

  • Innovation: Pushing boundaries for better solutions.
  • Collaboration: Fostering teamwork and partnerships.
  • User Delight: Sparking joy and excitement for travel.

TLDR - Solutions

Select What Matters to YOU!

Problem: Different groups have different constraints, filters, and preferences.

Solution: Users select what matters to them, and our AI suggests trip options while keeping the user in control.

Why: Current apps force users to input details they often don't know yet. They need ballpark ideas first!

Notable user quote: “I am always the organizer of trips. I always look at special details for trips like activities for kids, budget of people I am traveling with, expectations families will have for accommodations...”

Get Tips While You Explore

Problem: Users look at many different sources to get vacation ideas.

Solution: As users browse continents or countries—often their first decision—they get tips and context to help complete the rest of the filters.

Why: Users receive research while building their trip.

Notable user quote: “When I plan a trip I look at multiple factors at the same time (flights, budget, what we want to do). The process can be time consuming and annoying. I edit my trips so many times.”

What if You Want to Edit Something?

Problem: Users often need to change one factor that affects many other aspects, and reevaluating flights, stays, and budgets is annoying.

Solution: Using natural language, users edit their trip with Babble the turtle. If there's a conflict, Babble prompts a chat to help solve it.

Why: Users need quick proof to see if the trip still works.

Notable user quote: “If my boyfriend can't get the time off work then I have to restart my research.”

Share Your Suggestions!

Problem: Organizers care deeply about details, but attendees just need a snapshot.

What: Create snapshots that communicate the big picture to attendees.

Why: Organizers need fast yes/no answers to book flights, stays, and activities.

Notable user quote: “I have organized ALL the trips between families over the last few years. I know that at the end I need to give them 2 or 3 options, but it's difficult to show them what the trip will look like.”
